# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Customers and purchasing teams compare fabric options for garment production. They need to understand fabric details, care requirements, available colors, and social proof quickly enough to decide whether to reserve, add to cart, or continue comparing.

## Product Purpose

Tee Culture helps buyers evaluate and source fabric for apparel projects. The product detail experience should make each fabric feel trustworthy and premium while keeping practical buying information easy to scan.

## Brand Personality

Clean, trustworthy, and premium. The interface should feel polished and spacious without hiding important product details behind decorative styling.

## Anti-references

Avoid cluttered marketplace product pages that overload the buyer with competing panels, noisy badges, and dense repeated cards.

## Design Principles

- Keep product imagery and buying decisions at the top of the hierarchy.
- Make technical fabric details easy to scan without making the page feel crowded.
- Use the yellow brand accent for meaningful state and action, not decoration.
- Prefer clear, familiar controls over invented interactions.
- Keep whitespace generous so the catalog feels premium rather than crowded.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use readable contrast, visible focus states, and keyboard-accessible interactions. Motion should be subtle and should not be required to understand the interface.
